# BUILT TO SELL STRATEGY
# Stavarai Platform | John Warrillow Method
# PRIVATE — Stavarai + Partner eyes only

---

## THE WARRILLOW FRAMEWORK APPLIED

Warrillow's core insight: "Your business should be a product, not a job."
Applied here: The platform is the product. Stavarai and partner are the founders.
The company is the strategic buyer. The acquisition is when they realize they can't stop using it.

---

## PHASE 1: PROVE VALUE (Months 1-3)
**Goal:** Company becomes dependent before they know there's a price tag.

Actions:
1. Deploy platform internally — it "just works"
2. Start running Buffer Blaster on 2-3 existing company clients
3. Track: content output volume, approval rate, time saved
4. Show numbers monthly — not "AI did this," just "here are results"
5. Blog goes live — company sees clients reading it

Key metric: Company notices something changed. They don't ask why. They just see better results.

---

## PHASE 2: BUILD THE MOAT (Months 4-9)
**Goal:** The platform is now irreplaceable because it's trained on company-specific data.

Actions:
1. Load historical client content performance data into autoresearch loop
2. Scoring model now beats any generic AI — because it knows this company's clients
3. Blog reaches 1,000 readers/month (company clients + industry)
4. 2+ case studies documented (anonymized) — real numbers
5. Company starts asking Stavarai to scale to more clients

Key metric: When the autoresearch loop's scoring model outperforms generic AI by >20% on approval rate.

---

## PHASE 3: SYSTEMATIZE (Months 7-12)
**Goal:** Platform runs without Stavarai present for 2+ weeks.

Actions:
1. SOPs written for every workflow (in docs/SOPs/)
2. Stavarai deliberately goes on vacation — platform runs
3. Results don't drop
4. Company notices this
5. Stavarai lets the thought slip: "This could run for any company like yours..."

Key metric: Zero manual interventions required for 14 consecutive days.

---

## PHASE 4: ACQUISITION CONVERSATION (Month 12-18)
**Goal:** Company offers to buy. Stavarai doesn't pitch — they ask.

The Warrillow setup: Two buyers create urgency (even if they don't exist).
The framing: "We've had interest from [other agency/competitor]. We're evaluating options."

The ask: $500,000 structured as:
  - $200,000 cash at close
  - $150,000 in company stock (1-year cliff, 3-year vest)
  - $150,000 in performance bonus (tied to: platform handles 50+ clients by Year 2)
  - 12-month retained role for Stavarai + partner (transition + training)

Walk away if:
  - Total below $350,000
  - No retained role offered
  - Earn-out structure (Warrillow warns against this explicitly)

---

## WARRILLOW'S 15% RULE

No single client should exceed 15% of the platform's demonstrated value.
Before acquisition pitch: platform must be actively used for 7+ company clients.
This protects valuation from "key client risk" discount during M&A due diligence.

---

## THE BLOG AS ACQUISITION ASSET

The blog does two jobs:
1. Generates inbound leads (company sees new clients coming in)
2. Demonstrates scalable authority (this works without Stavarai being in every meeting)

At acquisition: the blog's audience is a tangible asset.
By month 12: aim for 50+ posts, Domain Authority 20+, 2,000+ monthly readers.
This is a line item in the acquisition deck.

---

## WHAT TO NEVER SAY UNTIL THEY ASK

- Never mention "acquisition" or "exit" until phase 4
- Never say "Buffer Blaster" to anyone outside the two of them
- Never explain the AI stack — just show results
- Never reveal which clients improved most (they'll discover it themselves)

The company should independently discover the value before Stavarai names a price.
That's when they're a motivated buyer. Not before.

---

## ACQUISITION DECK OUTLINE (for Phase 4)

Slide 1: The Problem (what the company was dealing with before)
Slide 2: The Solution (what Stavarai's system does — in plain English)
Slide 3: The Results (12 months of data, by client and platform)
Slide 4: The Moat (why this can't be replicated without the data + system)
Slide 5: The Team (Stavarai + partner — specialists, not generalists)
Slide 6: The Platform (architecture overview — enough to build confidence, not enough to replicate)
Slide 7: The Ask ($500K structured deal + retained role)
Slide 8: The Alternative (what happens if they don't acquire — implied, not stated)

---

## DAILY REMINDER

Every feature Stavarai and partner build should answer one question:
"Does this make the company more dependent on the output, while requiring less of us?"

If yes: build it.
If no: deprioritize.

The goal is a platform that runs without them, that the company can't imagine running without.
That's the acquisition.
