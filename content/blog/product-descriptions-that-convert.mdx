Most Shopify product descriptions try to do four jobs at once: describe the product, list the specs, tell a story, and close the sale. When you cram all of that into a paragraph, the customer reads none of it.

We've been testing a three-part structure across food and beauty brands, and the add-to-cart rate moves every time. It looks like this:

**Line one — the outcome.** What the customer gets, in plain words. Not the ingredients. Not the history. The result.

> *Wake up to skin that already looks rested.*

**Line two — the reason it works.** One sentence. The mechanism, not the marketing.

> *Niacinamide at 5% fades dark spots over four weeks.*

**Line three — the permission to buy.** Why now, or why this one over the cheaper alternative.

> *Used morning and night. Fragrance-free. 30-day return if it doesn't work for you.*

That's it. The specs go in an accordion below. The story goes in a reviews widget. The sale happens in those three lines.

## Why it works

The eye-tracking research on e-commerce pages is brutal. Customers spend about 15 seconds on a product page, and most of that goes to the first image and the price. The description gets a glance — maybe two seconds.

Two seconds is enough to read three lines. It is not enough to read a paragraph. When you front-load the outcome and the proof, the customer gets what they need before they decide to scroll.

## What to cut

If your current description contains any of these, cut them:

- Adjectives that describe your brand, not the product (*premium*, *luxurious*, *curated*)
- Exclamation marks
- Any sentence that starts with "Our"
- Feature lists presented as paragraphs (move them to bullets or an accordion)

The hard part isn't writing the three lines. It's having the discipline to delete everything else.
