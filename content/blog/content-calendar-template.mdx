We've tried every content calendar tool. Notion, Airtable, Trello, Later, Buffer's built-in planner. They all work. The problem was never the tool — it was the structure.

Here's the cadence we landed on after two years of testing. It assumes one brand, four platforms, and a realistic production capacity.

## The weekly rhythm

**Monday — review.** Look at last week's performance. Pick the three posts that overperformed. Write one sentence each on why. This is the input for the whole week.

**Tuesday — ideation.** 15 hooks across the four platforms. Scored and ranked. The bottom 10 get cut. The top 5 become the week's batch.

**Wednesday — production.** Shoot, source, or generate. Batch everything into one session. Context-switching between ideation and production is where the day disappears.

**Thursday — editing + scheduling.** Cut, format for each platform, schedule for the optimal windows. Done by end of day so Friday isn't a scramble.

**Friday — buffer.** Whatever didn't fit, plus a slot for reactive content (something that broke in the niche this week).

## Platform cadence (per week)

| Platform | Posts/week | Why |
|---|---|---|
| TikTok | 5–7 | Rewards frequency; fast decay |
| Instagram Reels | 4–5 | Mid-frequency; mid-decay |
| Pinterest | 7–10 | Rewards consistency; slow decay |
| Stories | daily | Maintain presence; low production cost |

The total looks high (20–25/week) but production is batched into Wednesday, so it's one shoot day, not seven.

## The scheduling windows that actually matter

These shift by niche, but our defaults:

- **Food & Beverage** — 11am–1pm (lunch), 5pm–7pm (dinner prep)
- **Beauty** — 7am–9am (morning routine), 8pm–10pm (wind-down scrolling)
- **Apparel** — 12pm–2pm (lunch break), 7pm–9pm (evening)
- **Home** — weekdays 8pm–10pm, Saturday 9am–11am

Don't trust these blindly. Pull your own analytics and confirm. But they're a better starting point than "post at noon."

## Why this beats a tool

The calendar isn't the spreadsheet. It's the rhythm. If your team knows that Monday is review, Tuesday is ideation, Wednesday is production — the tool becomes irrelevant. You could run this on a whiteboard.

We use Buffer for scheduling and a simple table for planning. The magic isn't in either. It's in never planning content on Sunday night ever again.
