The job description every Shopify brand posts looks the same:

> *Content Manager — must be skilled in TikTok, Instagram Reels, Pinterest, copywriting, photography, and video editing. $55k–$70k.*

That person does not exist. Not at that salary, and honestly not at any salary. The skill set you're describing is five jobs, and combining them into one role is why your content output is inconsistent.

## The math you're avoiding

A serious content operation for one brand needs, per week:

- **Research** — finding what's trending in the niche, what competitors are doing, what hooks are working (~6 hours)
- **Writing** — hooks, captions, scripts, blog (~8 hours)
- **Production** — shooting, sourcing, or generating video and images (~12 hours)
- **Editing** — cutting, captioning, formatting for each platform (~10 hours)
- **Scheduling and reporting** (~4 hours)

That's 40 hours for one brand, done well. If your content manager has three brands, something gets done badly or late. Usually it's the part that needed the most care.

## The honest options

**Option 1 — Hire a team.** A writer, a producer, an editor. Real cost: $180k–$250k all-in. Most brands can't justify this until they're past $5M.

**Option 2 — Lower your output.** Post less, but make it better. This works if your niche rewards quality over frequency. Beauty and apparel often don't.

**Option 3 — Automate the making, keep the human for the judging.** This is the path we took. The research, the writing, the production, the scheduling — that's 34 of those 40 hours, and most of it is pattern work. A system can do it. The 6 hours of judgment — *is this on brand, does this hook actually work, is this the right call* — that stays human.

## What you're actually buying when you hire

You're not buying output. You can buy output cheaper. You're buying taste, and taste is the part that doesn't scale across four platforms at 40 hours a week.

If you hire one person to do everything, you get their taste diluted across too much output. If you automate the output and pay one person to judge it, you get their taste concentrated where it matters.

That's the whole argument. It's not about replacing people. It's about putting the human on the decision that decides whether the work ships.
